Length is a fundamental physical quantity that measures the extent or distance between two points in space. It is a fundamental dimension and plays a vital role in various fields, including science, engineering, construction, and more.


Weight refers to the measure of the gravitational force acting on an object due to its mass. It is a fundamental property of matter and is commonly measured in units such as pounds (lb) or kilograms (kg).


Temperature is a fundamental physical quantity that measures the level of heat energy of the particles within a substance or system. In scientific terms, temperature is often defined based on the behavior of a particular substance, such as the expansion or contraction.


Scientists define energy as the ability to do work or cause change. It is present in various forms, such as kinetic energy, potential energy, thermal energy, and more.


Currency is a medium of exchange that facilitates the buying, selling, and trading of goods and services. It plays a crucial role in the global economy, and different countries have their own currencies.
