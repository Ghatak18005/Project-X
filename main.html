Length is a fundamental physical quantity that measures the extent or distance between two points in space. It is a fundamental dimension and plays a vital role in various fields, including science, engineering, construction, and everyday life. In scientific terms, length is typically defined as the measurement of the physical separation or spatial interval between two objects or points. It is often represented using units such as meters (m), centimeters (cm), inches (in), feet (ft), yards (yd), or miles (mi), among others, depending on the system of measurement used.


Weight refers to the measure of the gravitational force acting on an object due to its mass. It is a fundamental property of matter and is commonly measured in units such as pounds (lb) or kilograms (kg). Weight can be understood in relation to mass, but they are not the same. Mass is a measure of the amount of matter present in an object and is a constant property that remains the same regardless of the location or environment. In contrast, weight varies depending on the strength of the gravitational field acting upon an object. Weight is calculated by multiplying an object's mass by the acceleration due to gravity.


Temperature is a fundamental physical quantity that measures the level of heat or coldness of an object or environment. It is a measure of the average kinetic energy of the particles within a substance or system. In scientific terms, temperature is often defined based on the behavior of a particular substance, such as the expansion or contraction of a liquid, gas, or solid. The most commonly used temperature scale is the Celsius scale (°C), where water freezes at 0°C and boils at 100°C at standard atmospheric pressure. Another widely used scale is the Fahrenheit scale (°F), commonly used in the United States, where water freezes at 32°F and boils at 212°F at standard atmospheric pressure.


Scientists define energy as the ability to do work. Modern civilization is possible because people have learned how to change energy from one form to another and then use it to do work. People use energy to walk and bicycle, to move cars along roads and boats through water, to cook food on stoves, to make ice in freezers, to light our homes and offices, to manufacture products, and to send astronauts into space.


Currency is a medium of exchange that facilitates the buying, selling, and trading of goods and services. It serves as a unit of account, a store of value, and a standard of deferred payment. Throughout history, various forms of currency have been used by different civilizations, ranging from shells and beads to precious metals like gold and silver. Today, most countries have their own national currencies, which are issued and regulated by their respective central banks.
